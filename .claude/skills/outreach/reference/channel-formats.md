# Channel Formats

Each channel has its own rhythm. Generating a LinkedIn-style message for SMS will feel wrong. Match the medium.

## LinkedIn DM

- Conversational, slightly more professional than Instagram but still casual.
- 3-5 short sentences max.
- No bullet points. No headers. Just text.
- Open with their name, then the acknowledge, then the ask.
- Absolutely no "We help X do Y" openers. If you see yourself writing "we've been helping," delete and start over.

**Good:**
> Jake! Saw you made senior analyst, congrats man. Three years in and they're already moving you up, clearly doing something right. You liking the finance world or is it as brutal as everyone says?

**Bad:**
> Hey Jake, by the way we've been helping finance firms cut their operational costs by 40% using Voice AI. I'd be glad to show you.

## Instagram DM

- Even more casual than LinkedIn. Emojis are fine but not required.
- Reply to a story if possible, higher reply rates than cold DMs.
- 2-4 short sentences.

**Good:**
> Amy! Saw your story about the half marathon, that's insane. When did you get into running?

## SMS / WhatsApp

- Shortest. One or two sentences max.
- SMS ideally under 160 characters.
- WhatsApp can be slightly longer but still tight.
- Start with "Hey [name], it's [your name]" if there's any chance they don't have your number saved.

**Good (SMS):**
> Hey Tom, it's [your name] from down the street. Saw the new deck you built, looks really good. You do that yourself?

**Good (WhatsApp):**
> Yo Chris! Saw you're coaching youth soccer now, that's dope. How'd you get into that?

## Facebook Messenger

- Like WhatsApp. Casual. If it's someone you haven't spoken to in a while, a short "hey, it's been ages" line is fine before the ACA.

**Good:**
> Hey David! We met at Sarah and Tom's wedding last year. How do you know them again?

## Email (warm)

- Subject: short, human, NOT a sell. Something like "quick hello" or "[shared context]", e.g., "college days."
- Body: 3-4 sentences. Same ACA structure.
- No corporate formatting. No signature block beyond your name.
- Definitely no HTML templates. No "Hi {{firstName}}."

**Good:**
> Subject: quick hello
>
> Hey Jake, been a minute. Saw on LinkedIn you made senior analyst at the firm, congrats. Three years in and they're already promoting you, that's moving fast. How's finance been treating you?
>
> [your name]

## Phone Call

- You call the person directly. No script to send.
- Opening: "Hey [name], it's [your name]. How's it going?" Then let them ask what's up.
- Follow the same ACA mentally: acknowledge, compliment, ask, but spoken, not typed.
- You STILL don't lead with what you do. They ask what you're up to, you say "I actually started a business doing X outcome for businesses, pretty random I know."

## Voice Note (WhatsApp / Instagram)

- Great for people you actually know well. Feels personal.
- Same ACA structure, spoken casually.
- Keep it under 30 seconds.

## What NEVER Changes (across channels)

- Lead with their name.
- Acknowledge + Compliment + Ask.
- No product mention.
- No merge tags.
- No em dashes.
- Reads like you catching up, not you selling.

## Anti-Patterns (Real Examples from Students)

These are real messages people have sent. All are BROKEN.

**Broken #1 (cold pitch leak on LinkedIn, labeled warm):**
> Hey Vigny, by the way we have been helping real estate agencies to cut their costs and time and increase efficiency by using Voice AI, whatsapp and crm automations, solving their big pain point of leaking and manual uploading of leads in the crm. I'd be glad to show you.

Why broken: No acknowledge, no compliment, no question. Straight product sell. Also "we've been helping X" is the classic cold-email opener. This repo has no pitching anywhere in the methodology, so any draft that reads like this gets rewritten.

**Broken #2 (cold email template):**
> Sub: Your front desk still chasing no-shows?
> Hey {{firstName}}, Talked to a dentist last week who said: "Half my cancellations could've been saved if we just called back faster." We built a voice AI that calls + reschedules those patients, instantly, no staff needed. One 3-chair clinic in Dallas got back $8.7K in missed revenue month one. Want to see if it'd work at {{companyName}} too?

Why broken: Merge tags. Dentist hook. Case study stat. "Want to see..." close. This is cold outbound, not warm. If the user gives you this, refuse and regenerate.

**Broken #3 (serial follow-up with no ACA):**
> Hi Herky how is it going??
> Hey man
> Hey man
> Hey man
> We have been helping real estate agencies to cut their time and costs...

Why broken: Repeated pings with no content, then when they finally wrote something it was a cold sell. The solution isn't more pings, it's a real acknowledge + compliment on the first try.
