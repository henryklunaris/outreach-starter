# Sales Principles (when the conversation turns to your offer)

These principles apply AFTER the warm conversation has reached the point where they ask "so what are you up to?" They do not apply to first messages. First messages are ACA only.

**Important vocabulary note.** In this repo there is no "pitch". Ever. The two legitimate actions are **showing off what you do** (the Stage-1 answer when they ask, plus the demo) and **the referral ask**. If you find yourself reaching for the word "pitch" while reading this file, swap it mentally for "the framing" or "how you answer". The methodology has no pitching mode.

## 1. The Information Funnel

Different stages of the conversation get different amounts of information. Most people get this wrong by dumping everything into the first message.

**Stage 1, First mention (they asked what you do):**
Outcome only. No mechanism, no tech, no details.

Good: "I actually started my own business building AI voice systems for businesses. Pretty random career move honestly."

Bad: "I build voice AI agents on Vapi with custom tool calls and webhook integrations that handle inbound call routing..."

**Stage 2, They're curious / you sent a demo:**
Outcome + high-level mechanism + proof if you have it.

Good: "Basically we set up AI on the phone line that picks up calls when the team can't, qualifies the lead, and books the appointment. Works especially well for places that miss calls after hours or during lunch."

**Stage 3, On a discovery call:**
Full mechanism, pricing, onboarding, integrations, everything. Now they want to know how you deliver.

## 2. New Money vs Scalping

Cold prospects don't buy optimization. They buy new money.

- **Scalping framing (dies)**: "AI receptionist that answers your calls 24/7" / "Reduce your front desk workload" / "Stop paying your receptionist."
- **New money framing (works)**: "We'll get you 15 new emergency jobs per month that would normally go to competitors, booked straight into your calendar."

The voice agent is the mechanism. The product is new revenue they weren't getting before.

For warm contacts, this shows up when you pivot from catchup to showing what you do. You're not saying "I automate receptionists." You're saying "I help businesses capture money they're currently losing to missed calls and slow response times."

## 3. Expansion, Not Replacement

Never position AI as "replace your receptionist." It triggers every defense mechanism a business owner has (loyalty to staff, fear of change, skepticism).

Instead:
- "Handle the calls your team can't get to."
- "Capture the revenue you're currently losing."
- "Your best people shouldn't be stuck answering routine calls."

## 4. The Four Easiest Use Cases (rank order)

When suggesting what to offer, default to these:

1. **Speed-to-Lead**, clients with paid ads. Their leads get called back in 24 hours, by then already called 3 competitors. Your AI calls back in 60 seconds. Results in 1-2 weeks.
2. **After-Hours Call Handling**, any service business. Missed calls at night/weekends = competitor wins the job. Results in 1-2 weeks, safest option.
3. **Database Reactivation**, any business with old leads in a CRM. Even 5% conversion on 500 dead leads = serious recovered revenue. Highest ROI. Results in 2-3 weeks.
4. **No-Show Reduction**, appointment-based businesses (dental, medspa). 20% no-show rate = thousands per month gone. Results in 1 week, simplest build.

DO NOT frame around: full AI receptionist replacing front desk (too many edge cases), outbound cold calling agents (compliance nightmare), complex multi-step workflows (too much to break).

## 5. Clarity > Jargon

If your prospect has to decode what you said, they delete it. You suffer from Curse of Knowledge: you live in this world, they don't.

- Confusing: "I built an AI voice agent demo for your backend."
- Clear: "I made a 2-min recording of an AI system that can handle 60% of your inbound calls without your team touching them."

Say the outcome in plain English. Save the jargon for Stage 3.

## 6. The Anti-Hype Opener (for saturated markets)

If the contact happens to be a business owner, they're getting AI stuff thrown at them every day. Acknowledge it:

> "I know you probably get AI tools thrown at you constantly. Honestly most of them are terrible and full of empty promises. I'm not here to sell you hype, just wanted to show you what actually works."

This pattern-interrupts the "another AI guy" reflex and positions you next to them, not against them. Remember: this isn't a pitch, you're showing them what you built and they can decide.

## 7. Trust Without Case Studies

New people don't have case studies. That's fine. Other ways to build trust:

1. Run a discovery process so good they assume you've done it 100 times (ask about THEIR business, THEIR numbers, THEIR process).
2. Build a demo that's actually impressive (handle a nightmare scenario, show CRM integration live, Miro flowchart of the full system).
3. Use third-party research ("Harvard study on 60-second response time increasing conversions up to 20x").
4. Create a proof asset (simulate 1000 conversations, score them, report 93% accuracy).
5. Acknowledge the skepticism (Anti-Hype Opener, above).
6. Show your exact process (Day 1-12 onboarding plan).
7. Structure the offer so case studies don't matter (Free Implementation, Free AI Audit, Performance-Only, client risks $0-200, you risk your time).

## 8. Low-Risk Offers That Work

- **Free Implementation**: client covers $50 in expenses (API credits + phone number), you deliver free for 2 weeks. If they see value, they pay to keep it.
- **Free AI Audit**: rebranded discovery. 2-3 calls to analyze their business and propose an AI solution. Positions you as expert.
- **Performance-Only**: client pays only for expenses. You get paid per booked appointment or per scheduled lead. Don't do "per closed deal", keep it on leading metrics.

## 9. The Referral Ask

Most warm outreach conversations don't close the person. They unlock referrals. After you've shown a demo (or they've gone silent):

> "Yeah if anyone comes to mind who might benefit from this, I'd appreciate the intro. Could be contractors, shop owners, small medical practices, anyone who misses calls a lot."

Then, if they say no one comes to mind:

> "All good. What about someone you don't like?" (lightens the mood, sometimes gets a real answer)

92% of people trust referrals from friends/family. 91% of customers would give referrals if asked. Most never get asked.

## 10. Volume and Consistency Beat Talent

Most people who fail at warm outreach haven't done the volume.

**Daily target is the Golden 100.**

- **First 10 contacts ever = the warm-up ritual.** This is just to break through the awkwardness of messaging people you know. Nothing magic about 10, it's the minimum viable friction-breaker.
- **After the warm-up, target 100 per day** until discovery calls are landing. Not 10. One hundred. Consistency beats everything. Every no gets you closer to a yes.
