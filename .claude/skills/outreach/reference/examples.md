# ACA Outreach Examples

Six real scenarios covering different relationships and platforms. Use these as pattern references when generating new messages. Match the tone and structure to the channel and relationship.

## Scenario 1: High School Friend (Instagram DM)

**Opening:**
> Amy! Saw your story about the half marathon, that's insane. When did you get into running?

**First response:** "Started back when covid was around..."

**ACA reply:**
- Acknowledge: "Started during lockdown and stuck with it, respect."
- Compliment: "Most people gave up after like 2 weeks, you actually committed."
- Ask: "You doing another one or taking a break?"

**When they ask "what about you?":** "nm honestly haha, tell me more about the running though, you training for another one?"

**When they push "no but seriously what are you up to these days?":** "Pretty good actually! Left my job and started something that helps businesses not miss calls. Pretty niche but it's been working."

**They want to know more:** "Honestly easier to show than explain, want me to send you a quick demo? it's like 2 mins"

**After they watch the demo and reply ("that's actually cool"):** "right? yeah it's been fun building it. appreciate you checking it out"

**Referral ask (separate message, after they've acknowledged the demo):** "hey random but if anyone comes to mind who might find that useful, would appreciate the intro. cheers"

## Scenario 2: College Acquaintance (LinkedIn)

**Opening:** "Jake! Saw you made senior analyst, congrats man. How long you been at that firm?"

**ACA:**
- Acknowledge: "Three years and already promoted, that's moving fast."
- Compliment: "You're clearly doing something right if they're moving you up."
- Ask: "You like the finance world or is it as brutal as everyone says?"

**When they ask "what about you, what are you up to?":** "Honestly not bad, just been busy with stuff. How's the finance world treating you though, is it as brutal as everyone says?"

**When they circle back "no but what's the new thing you're doing?":** "Yeah things are good! I actually started a business that helps companies catch calls they'd otherwise miss. Pretty random I know."

**They respond "Wait what? How does that work lol":** "Haha yeah it sounds weird. Honestly easier to show than explain, I've got a quick demo I can send if you wanna see it?"

**After they watch and reply:** Don't ask for referral yet. Let them react. If they go quiet, follow up in 3-5 days about something else entirely (their job, weekend plans, whatever). Only ask for referrals after they've acknowledged the demo or after 2+ weeks of silence.

## Scenario 3: Neighbor (Text Message)

**Opening:** "Hey Tom, it's [name] from down the street. Saw the new deck you built, looks really good. You do that yourself?"

**ACA:**
- Acknowledge: "Built it yourself over two weekends, that's dedication."
- Compliment: "Most people would've just hired someone, but you actually know what you're doing."
- Ask: "You do a lot of DIY projects or was this a one-off?"

**When he asks how you're doing:** "Can't complain honestly. You still doing DIY stuff or was the deck a one-off?"

**When he asks more directly "so what are you doing for work these days?":** "Actually quit my job and started something new, basically help businesses not miss phone calls. Big change but it's been interesting."

**He replies "Wait really? How does that work?":** "Kinda hard to explain in text honestly, I've got a quick demo I can send if you wanna see it? Pretty quick to watch" [send link]

**After he watches and replies ("that's pretty cool actually"):** "Yeah it's been fun. Appreciate you checking it out man."

**Referral ask (separate message, only after he's acknowledged the demo):** "Hey random question, you know any small business owners around here? Contractors, shop owners, anyone really. If anyone comes to mind who hates missing calls, I'd owe you one."

## Scenario 4: Person You Met at a Wedding Once (Facebook)

**Opening:** "Hey David! We met at Sarah and Tom's wedding last year. How do you know them again?"

**ACA:**
- Acknowledge: "College buddies, makes sense you were at the wedding then."
- Compliment: "Cool that you guys stayed close after all these years."
- Ask: "You still in Chicago or did you move?"

**When conversation continues ("Yeah still in Chicago, what about you?"):** "Nice, I'm still in [city]. How's work going for you?"

**When they eventually ask what you're up to:** "Yeah I'm doing well actually. Started my own thing recently, basically a service that helps businesses not lose customers to missed calls. Pretty niche but interesting."

**They want to know more:** "Honestly easier to show than explain, I can send you a quick demo if you're curious? Takes like 2 mins."

**After they watch and reply:** Let them react first. If they go quiet, follow up in 3-5 days about something else entirely. Only ask for referrals after they've acknowledged the demo.

**Referral ask (once they've acknowledged):** "If anyone comes to mind who might find it useful, would appreciate the intro. No pressure at all."

**If no:** "No worries at all. Maybe someone that you hate then?"

## Scenario 5: Former Roommate (WhatsApp)

**ACA:**
- Acknowledge: "Volunteer coaching on top of your regular job, that's commitment."

- Ask: "The kids any good or total chaos?"

**When he asks about you ("haha what about you, what you been up to?"):** "Yeah man things are solid, just been busy with stuff. How's the actual job going though, you still at [place]?"

**When he pushes ("yeah but what's the new thing you're doing?"):** "Haha fair, yeah I started something that helps businesses catch calls they'd normally miss. Wild learning curve but it's been cool."

**He wants to know more:** "Honestly easier to show than explain, want me to send you a quick demo?"

**After he watches and replies ("that's actually sick"):** "Yeah it's been fun honestly. Appreciate you checking it out man."

**Referral ask (separate message, after he's acknowledged):** "Random question, you know anyone who might find that useful? Parents of the kids you coach, people at your gym, whatever. If anyone comes to mind I'd owe you one."

**If no:** "All good. What about someone you really don't like?"

## Scenario 6: Your Mum's Friend from Book Club (Text)

**Opening:** "Hey Jennifer, it's [your name], [your mum's name]'s son. She mentioned you're loving retirement now, that's awesome. How are you spending all that free time?"

**ACA:**
- Acknowledge: "Volunteering and traveling, sounds like you're busier than when you worked."
- Compliment: "Most people just sit around, you're actually doing stuff."
- Ask: "Where's the next trip?"

**When she asks what you're doing:** "Thanks for asking! Things are good, just been busy. How's retirement treating you though, you loving it?"

**When she circles back ("yes but what's the new business?"):** "Oh yeah, I started something that helps businesses catch calls they'd normally miss. Pretty different from what I was doing before but it's been great."

**She wants to know more:** "Honestly easier to show than explain, I can send you a quick demo if you're curious? Takes about 2 mins."

**After she watches and replies ("oh that's clever!"):** "Thank you! Yeah it's been fun building it honestly."

**Referral ask (separate message, after she's acknowledged):** "Random question, can you think of anyone who might find that useful? Anyone really. If someone comes to mind I'd appreciate the intro."

**If no:** "No worries! Anyone who drives you crazy?"

## Why This Works

You don't know who someone knows until you talk to them. Your mum's friend from book club isn't a "business owner," but she might have a son running a clinic. Your old flatmate might coach a team whose parents own businesses. Your cousin might work next to someone whose dad runs a plumbing company.

Niche doesn't matter for warm outreach. Business-owner status doesn't matter for warm outreach. The only thing that matters is that the person knows your name. From there, the referral does the rest.
