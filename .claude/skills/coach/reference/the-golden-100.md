# The Golden 100

The daily volume target for this methodology. Two phases.

## Phase 1, the warm-up (first 10 contacts ever)

The first 10 warm contacts you message are a ritual, not a target. Their job is to break the psychological wall of "can I actually send this". Once you've sent ten, the eleventh is mechanically easier than the first was. The awkwardness doesn't disappear, but it stops being paralyzing.

Don't try to hit Golden 100 on day one. Hit 10 total, real, thoughtful messages to real warm contacts, and log them. Then keep going.

If you can't send the first 10, no amount of drafting sophistication is going to help. Talk to `/coach` and read `mindset-deep.md`.

## Phase 2, the Golden 100

Once you're past the warm-up, the target is 100 per day until discovery calls are landing. Not 10. One hundred.

### What counts as one of the 100

- A new outbound warm message (first-touch ACA).
- A follow-up message to a silent contact.
- A reply to an inbound message from a warm contact.
- A referral ask sent.
- A conversation move that advances the thread toward a demo or referral.

What doesn't count:

- "Hey" with no substance.
- Internal pings between you and the AI drafting tool.
- Sending yourself the demo link to test it.

### Why 100

Activation energy compounds. At 100 a day you're sending a message every 5-10 minutes during a work day. The awkwardness has no room to build up. At 10 a day you agonize over each one.

100 also surfaces real volume signal. Out of 100 you'll get 10-25 meaningful replies, 2-5 referrals, 1-2 demo requests. At 10 a day you can't tell if you're doing it right or not.

### Consistency over perfection

Missing a day is fine. Skipping a week is where this dies. The tracker logs your counts, the SessionStart hook surfaces them back. If you see "3 / 100" at the start of a session, that's a signal to block 45 minutes before anything else.

### Your realistic ramp

Golden 100 is the aspiration, not a day-one expectation. If `/activate` captured that you have 30 minutes a day, your realistic daily volume is 30 to 45 messages, not 100, and that's fine. You ramp to the Golden 100 over time as:

- The drafting gets faster with practice (first messages take 5 min each, by week 2 they take 1).
- You carve out more time once a few early wins make it feel worth the tradeoff.
- Discovery calls start booking, which usually reshuffles the calendar anyway.

Time-strapped student math: a 9-to-5 with kids typically means 30-60 min a day of usable outreach time. At 1-2 messages per minute (most are follow-ups or replies, not cold first messages), that's 30-120 messages a day achievable. Start at the low end, build from there.

What's NOT okay is using availability as a reason to not start. 20 minutes a day consistently beats 0. The SessionStart hook will show you both numbers (Golden 100 target + your realistic ceiling) so you can see both the aspiration and the reality at once.

## Running out before hitting 100

Two possibilities:

- You have more warm contacts than you think. See `small-network.md`.
- You've genuinely exhausted the list. See `running-out.md` and go to the Alexander course.

Don't invent cold outreach inside this repo to hit the number. That's a different game with different rules. Switching to cold without the right tooling will produce worse results than stopping at 40 warm a day.
