# Demo Examples

Coach-approved reference demos. Use these as anchors when a student asks "what should my demo look like" or "what are good examples I can show friends to understand what this actually sounds like".

If a student doesn't have their own demo yet and wants to warm someone up with a concrete example before they build their own, they can share one of these links directly (honest framing: "here's the kind of thing I'm building, basically this, it's too hard to explain so its easier to just show").

## Top examples (in order coach usually shares them)

### 1. Candidate application screening

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=myyMRFMTKoo

Use case: inbound AI voice screener for job applications. Good first example because the "business owner hiring" pain point is intuitive for anyone, even non-business folks.

### 2. Real estate inbound

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=98c-kI_GpFc

Use case: general real estate inbound handling. Solid for students whose warm contacts include agents, property managers, or anyone on the real estate side.

### 3. Real estate qualifier

https://youtu.be/GHB6uMR2ABs

Use case: qualifying inbound real estate leads before they go to an agent. More advanced than #2, shows the "filters the call before you touch it" value.

### 4. Speech-to-speech real estate

https://youtu.be/-ePBCITr4ec

Use case: shows off the latency and natural flow of speech-to-speech. Best demo for when someone asks "does it actually sound human" or "how responsive is it".

## How to use these in a warm conversation

Only AFTER goodwill is built, never in message 1. When the natural "so what are you up to?" moment happens and they ask what it actually sounds like:

> "Easier to show than explain, want me to send a 2-min demo?"

Pick the example that matches their world (but if none match just tell them to pick their favorite one for now, as the idea is to show off the idea that it's an AI speaking, nothing else):

- They run or work in real estate → #2 or #3.
- They hire / run an agency with job postings → #1.
- Tech-leaning person who'd appreciate the latency show-off → #4.
- Generic "just curious what AI voice sounds like" → #4 or 1 are good and safest broad-appeal.

If the student asks which one to send first to a given contact, route them through this list based on the contact's vertical. Don't overthink, one link is enough per conversation.

## Note for the student

These are the coach's examples, borrow them if you need to before you've built your own. But as soon as you have your own demo (even a rough one from ElevenLabs or Retell AI), switch to that. "Here's one I built" always beats "here's one my coach built".
