# No Niches in Warm Outreach

This is doctrine. If a student asks "what niche should I pick for warm outreach", the answer is: you don't. That's what makes it warm.

## Why no niches

A niche is a filter on a list of strangers. You need a niche when you're cold-prospecting because you have to buy lists, write offers, target campaigns. The economics only work if you pick a slice and go deep.

Warm outreach inverts the whole thing. The list isn't strangers, it's people who know your name. You can't filter it by industry without cutting most of your list out. Your auntie isn't in any niche. Your old flatmate works at a firm that isn't in any niche.

And the goal isn't to close the warm contact anyway. The goal is to get referrals. Your auntie's friend's son runs a dental practice, you'd never know that until you asked her.

Niche-picking in warm is a category error.

## Why students try to niche anyway

Two reasons:

1. **Niche-picking is a great stall tactic.** Spending two weeks "researching" whether to target dentists or contractors means two weeks of not doing the scary thing, which is messaging people they know. The niche debate eats time that should have been spent on 50 warm conversations.
2. **Cold outreach courses online have niche as step one**, and students assume warm works the same way. It doesn't.

## What about the dentist / contractor / gym examples in the explainers?

Those examples exist to help you *describe the outcome* to someone who happens to own one of those businesses. They are about phrasing, not targeting. If your cousin runs a dental practice, you describe the outcome in dentist-world terms. That's not niching, that's translating.

Vertical examples in `.claude/skills/outreach/reference/explaining-voice-ai.md` are for this kind of translation, not for picking who to reach out to.

## When does niche matter?

When you graduate to cold outreach. That's where the Alexander course picks up. At that point you pick one vertical, one offer, one outcome, and you run a real campaign. But not before then. Don't cold-prospect while you still have warm contacts to message.

## What to say to a student obsessing over niche

> "park the niche question for now. message 30 people you know this week, no filtering. see who responds and who refers. come back to niche after, you'll have data."

They won't want to. Make them anyway.
