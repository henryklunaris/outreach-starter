# LinkedIn Is Full of Old Colleagues and Agency Owners, How Do I Handle Them?

Two of the most common stuck-points for VAIB students. Different answers for each.

## Old colleagues on LinkedIn

This is actually your best-case scenario, not a problem. Every old coworker who would recognize your name IS a warm contact.

Most students hesitate because "we haven't spoken in ages" feels awkward. But that's exactly what the acquaintance-tier full-ACA flow is for. It exists precisely for the 3-years-silent ex-colleague case.

**The rule**: apply the warmth test. Would they go "oh yeah, [your first name] from [place]" without extra context? If yes, warm. Message them.

**The failure mode**: treating them as "LinkedIn connections" (a list) instead of humans you worked alongside. The tell is when a student starts saying "I have 500 LinkedIn connections I could message", that's targeting logic, not warm logic. The real number is the subset who'd recognize them by name, and that's usually a few dozen.

**The practical pattern**: open `/outreach`, attach the old coworker's profile, tell Claude "old coworker, we haven't spoken in X years", answer the "how close were you actually" question honestly. The draft will land in acquaintance-with-thread or acquaintance-cold cell depending on whether you have an existing DM thread with them.

## Agency owners, and why most of them aren't referral sources

This is the trickier one. Students see "agency owner" on a LinkedIn profile and their brain flips from "do I know this person" to "is this person useful to me". That flip is the cold-outreach instinct wearing warm clothes, and it breaks the whole frame.

Two rules that keep it honest:

**Rule 1: Message them because you know them, not because they run an agency.**

If you wouldn't have reached out when they were just a random friend, don't reach out now because they're an agency owner. That pull is the target-market instinct, which warm outreach doesn't have.

**Rule 2: Same-vertical peers are not referral sources. Different-vertical peers are.**

This is the distinction that matters most.

- **Same vertical** (fellow voice-AI agency owners, fellow AI-automation agencies): NOT a referral source. They're chasing the same clients you are. If they get a lead that needs voice AI, they're keeping it. Asking them for referrals is like asking a competitor to hand you a sale. Treat these people as peers to stay in touch with, swap notes, maybe collaborate on something down the line, but don't expect referrals. The right mental model is "colleague in the same industry".
- **Different vertical** (marketing agency, dev agency, creative agency, SaaS founder who sells to SMBs): actually great referral sources. They have SMB clients who need voice AI, and they're not positioned to build it themselves, so they can refer without conflict. These are the agency-owner conversations worth having.

## How to message each

For a same-vertical peer you actually know:

> Hey Mark, been meaning to say, saw you're also deep in the voice AI thing now, crazy how fast this space has moved. You mostly doing inbound or outbound builds these days?

Zero referral ask. Just peer catchup. If anything useful comes out of it, great, but the goal is the relationship.

For a different-vertical agency owner who knows you:

> Hey Sarah! Wild how much has changed since [shared context]. You still running [their agency]? I've been building voice AI for small businesses, catching missed calls and booking appointments. Anyone in your client base who'd kill to have that? No pressure, just thought of you.

Referral ask fits naturally here because there's no conflict.

For an agency owner you DON'T actually know (LinkedIn-only connect):

Not warm. Don't message via this tool. That's cold outreach and it has different rules, different offer, different framework. Go to the Alexander course for cold.

## The underlying trap

If a student starts viewing LinkedIn as a targeting list ("who could I extract value from"), warm outreach is over. The correct view is "who do I actually know here, and how's their life going". Referrals are a byproduct of that, not the engine.
