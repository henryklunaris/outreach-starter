## Starter entries

### "Can I do this on behalf of someone else?"

No. Warm outreach has to come from the person whose name the contact recognizes. You can help draft, review, and coach, but the sender has to be the person with the relationship. A message sent on someone's behalf reads cold no matter how perfect the ACA.

### "Can I reuse the same first message structure across contacts?"

The structure yes (ACA is the structure). The specific words no. The acknowledge has to be about *them*, which means it changes per contact. If you find yourself copy-pasting the body of the message across 10 contacts, you've drifted into cold outreach.

### "Is it okay to reach out to an ex-client who ghosted me?"

Yes, if the relationship was real. Don't lead with the past deal. Lead with something you genuinely want to catch up on. If they re-engage, the work can come up naturally later. If they don't, accept it.

### "What if the person is mutual with a partner or roommate, not me?"

If you've met them enough times to use their first name and not explain who you are, warm. If you've only heard about them from your partner, not warm, but your partner could introduce. See `referral-ask.md` for intro mechanics.

### "What if I feel weird about using AI to draft?"

Everyone does at first. Two things to hold:
1. You're still reading, editing, and sending. The final message is yours.
2. The AI is there to reduce the "what do I say" friction, not to replace you. If the draft sounds like you, the fact that a tool helped is irrelevant to the recipient.

### "Can I add follow-up automation?"

No. This tool doesn't send. Automation would turn warm into cold. The follow-ups surface as reminders, you decide when and what. See `.claude/skills/outreach/SKILL.md` Step 6.
