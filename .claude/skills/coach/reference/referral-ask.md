# The Referral Ask

The actual revenue-generating action in warm outreach. Most students skip it or do it wrong.

## When to ask

Not in message one. Not after the first reply. Not before they've seen what you do.

Good timings:
- After you've sent the demo and they've acknowledged it ("yeah that's cool").
- If they've gone silent 1-2 weeks after the demo, one soft ask.
- At the natural end of a good catchup thread, even if they haven't seen the demo yet, if the vibe is genuinely warm.

Bad timings:
- In message one. Unearned.
- Before any catchup happened. Transactional.
- While the conversation is still about them. Breaks the ACA flow.

## How to ask

Keep it low-pressure, specific enough to trigger associations, and let them say no.

Generic:
> "yeah if anyone comes to mind who might benefit from this, would appreciate the intro."

Slightly more specific (usually lands better):
> "if anyone comes to mind, especially people who hate missing calls, contractors, shop owners, small medical practices, send them my way. i'd owe you one."

For a contact who's explicitly in or around a vertical:
> "yeah if anyone in [their world, restaurants / real estate / whatever] comes to mind, would love an intro. no pressure."

Notice: "i'd owe you one" or "really appreciate it" softens the ask and adds a tiny reciprocity signal without being weird.

## When they say "no one comes to mind"

Don't push. But one playful second ask often unlocks a real answer:

> "all good. what about someone you don't like?"

People laugh, and half the time they come back with a real name. The tone shift from "someone helpful" to "someone annoying" disarms the mental filter where they were only thinking of close friends.

## When they give you a name

Ask how to approach:

> "sweet, how do you know [them]? and is it better if i reach out directly or if you drop a quick line first?"

An introduction from the warm contact beats a direct outreach 10x every time. If they offer to introduce, take that offer. If they want you to reach out cold, ask for the context so your first message can reference them.

## When they refer and the referral ghosts

Thank the warm contact anyway. Seriously, a quick "hey appreciate the intro, they were cool, didn't end up going anywhere but thanks" keeps you in goodwill. The same person will refer you again 6 months later if you don't make the ghost feel like their fault.

## The philosophical frame

You are not asking for a favor. You are offering a service that might help someone they care about. Carnegie would point out that most people genuinely enjoy connecting friends with useful things, if the thing is actually useful. Your job is to make it easy for them to say yes (specific prompt, low pressure), and to make it easy for them to say no (no follow-up pressure if they don't have anyone).

If you don't ask, they can't refer. Most students never ask.
