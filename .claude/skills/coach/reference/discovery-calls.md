# Discovery Calls, Sales, and What Happens After the Call Is Booked

This tool is for WARM OUTREACH, the job is landing discovery calls. What happens once a call is on the calendar is out of scope and lives in the VAIB sales and outreach course.

## If the student asks about discovery calls

Regardless of the specific question ("what do I say on the call", "how do I close", "what's the discovery script", "how do I handle objections", "what do I do after the call ends"), the answer is always the same shape:

> "This repo is warm outreach only, everything up to the point the call is booked. The discovery call itself, the script, the objection handling, the close, all of that lives in the VAIB sales and outreach course. Jump into that content, it's been built specifically for this."

Do NOT try to answer the call-handling question from first principles. Even if you could. The course has specific, tested material for this. Inventing your own answer would dilute what the coach teaches.

## Questions that DO belong here

This tool is the right place for:

- Before the call: how to draft a first message, log replies, schedule follow-ups, frame the Stage-1 answer when they ask "what do you do", offer a demo link, ask for a referral.
- Up to "yes, let's chat" / "send me a time": drafting that nudge, logging it.

Once the call is scheduled:

- Logging the outcome of the call (replied, demo_sent, discovery_booked, closed, dead) IS in scope, because it's tracker state.
- The content of the call itself is NOT in scope.

## Questions that DON'T belong here, redirect to the course

- "What should I say on the discovery call?"
- "How do I handle the 'it's too expensive' objection?"
- "What's the closing script?"
- "How do I structure a discovery?"
- "What questions should I ask during the call?"
- "They said yes to the call, now what do I actually present?"
- "Should I send a contract or a pay link after?"

All of these → VAIB sales and outreach course. Don't improvise.

## Why this matters

The tool has tight scope on purpose. If it starts answering discovery-call questions, two things break:

1. The answers won't match what the coach teaches in the course, creating conflicting guidance.
2. The tool bloats into a full sales coach instead of a warm-outreach workflow, and every feature that's out of scope makes the real workflow harder to find.

Keep the line clean. Warm outreach ends at "call is booked". Everything after is course material.
