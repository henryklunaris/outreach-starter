# They Viewed the Demo But Didn't Reply

One of the most common stuck-points. The student sent the demo link, the contact clicked it (or even listened), and then silence.

## Reframe first

Silence isn't rejection. It's usually one of:
- They're genuinely busy and will reply later.
- They thought it was cool, moved on, and forgot to respond (this is the majority case).
- They're not sure what to say because there's no obvious next step. This is your fault, not theirs.
- They don't see the connection to anyone they know yet.

None of these require you to chase harder. None of them mean they hate it.

## Don't chase. Don't reference the demo.

A bad follow-up reminds them they owe you a reply and makes the demo the center of gravity. You already lost if you send "hey just wanted to check you saw the link". It puts them on the spot, and it's transactional.

A good follow-up moves the conversation somewhere else entirely, like a normal friendship.

## The 3-5 day follow-up

Wait 3-5 days. Then send a message that has nothing to do with the demo. Pick up something else from their life:

> "oi how was the weekend? you end up doing that trip?"

> "btw random but did you ever end up watching [thing you'd both talked about]"

> "hey was thinking about you, heard [mutual friend] is moving to [place], you still in touch?"

The point is to keep the friendship warm. They'll remember the demo. You don't need to remind them.

## If they bring it back up

If, on a later reply, they come back to "yeah the voice AI thing was wild", that's when you can move to the referral ask:

> "yeah appreciate you taking a look. if anyone comes to mind who'd find it useful, i'd owe you one. contractors, small medical practices, anyone who hates missing calls."

Don't force it. Let them bring it up.

## If it's been 2+ weeks of silence

One last soft referral ask is fair:

> "hey no pressure at all, but wanted to ask, anyone come to mind for that voice thing i showed you? even someone you kinda know. if not zero worries, just thought i'd ask."

Then drop it. If they say no one, say "all good, cheers" and move the conversation back to them. This isn't about closing them, it's about referrals, and the friendship matters more than any single ask.
