# "I Only Know 5 / 20 / 50 People, Is This Even Worth It For Me?"

Short answer: yes, and probably more worth it than for someone who knows 500.

## Why

Your mum's friend from book club doesn't know anything about voice AI. But her son runs a dental practice. Your uncle you haven't seen in three years coaches a youth team and the parents of those kids own businesses. Your flatmate from 2019 works at a gym whose owner misses half her inbound calls.

You are not trying to close the people you know. You are trying to get referrals from them into their networks. Every warm contact is a bridge to an unknown number of second-degree contacts that you will never reach via cold outreach.

20 well-executed warm conversations almost always beats 500 cold LinkedIn DMs.

## What to do with 20 people

Write the full list in a notes app. Names only. Don't filter. Don't pre-judge "oh they wouldn't care". You don't know what they know.

Then go through the list and for each person, answer: when was the last time we spoke, and what's something real I could acknowledge about them? If you genuinely can't think of either, park them and come back when you have context.

Target: 5-10 warm messages per day out of your 20, rotating. You'll get through the list in 3-4 days. Then follow up on threads, log replies, and ask for referrals once goodwill is there.

Once those 20 are moving (2-3 referrals usually come out by week 2), the list is no longer 20. It's 20 plus their referrals.

## What if I really know nobody

Rare. Actually listing names usually reveals 40-60 people most students forgot. Parents' friends, old coworkers, classmates, neighbors, gym regulars, flatmates, hobby-group people, people you went on trips with.

If after that exercise you genuinely have fewer than 15 names, the warm starter is going to run out faster for you. That's okay. Do the 15 properly, then graduate to the Alexander course on niche-picking and cold outreach. The fundamentals are the same, the channel changes.

## Quality always beats volume for small networks

For a student with 500 friends, 100 casual messages a day is the play. For a student with 30, slowing down and writing a thoughtful message to one specific person each time is the play. Don't force Golden-100 pace if your network doesn't support it, force the 100 of *quality reps* including follow-ups and referral conversations, not just first sends.
