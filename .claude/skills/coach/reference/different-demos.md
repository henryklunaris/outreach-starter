# Do I Send Different Demos to Different People?

Short answer: probably not. One good general demo is enough for 90% of warm conversations.

## Why one demo usually works

Warm contacts aren't evaluating your agency. They're doing you a favor by watching. They want to see "oh this is what my friend actually does, cool". They're not comparing you to a competitor or grading the fit.

So the demo's job is not to match their industry perfectly. Its job is to make what you do feel tangible. A two-minute clip of an AI picking up a call, asking a few questions, and handling some uncommon scenarios, or if they are sending this to their best mate, an AI that swears at them might be the type of thing that might get a couple laughs (just be careful, this should land with your audience).

The recipient will then either say "cool, not for me" and move on (that's fine, ask for a referral), or "wait, my cousin runs a [thing], she'd love this" (jackpot, that's the whole point).

## When a vertical-specific demo makes sense

The only time to reach for a different demo is when:

1. **The contact themselves runs the kind of business the demo targets.** If your friend runs a dental practice and you have a no-show reduction demo, send that one. That's not niching, that's matching the demo to their reality.
2. **They explicitly ask for something specific.** "Do you have one that handles booking?" yes send the booking one. Don't gatekeep.
3. **The general demo would sound weird in context.** If your demo is clearly of a real estate AI, and your contact is a restaurant owner, it's worth swapping or introducing it differently. The outcome is the same, the example has to feel coherent.

## What NOT to do

Don't build a custom demo per conversation. You'll lose the whole afternoon for one message. The quality of the draft matters far more than the specificity of the demo. Spend the time on the draft.

Don't change your one-liner answer for every person. The elevator answers in `.claude/skills/outreach/reference/explaining-voice-ai.md` work across verticals. Pick one that feels natural to you and reuse it.

Don't turn the demo choice into a stall tactic. "I need to build a better demo for [type of person]" is a form of avoidance. Your existing demo is good enough. Send it.

## Practical setup

Have one primary general demo (2 minutes, angry lunch landy, dealing with an emotional customer, receptionist-style, booking an appointment, sounds natural). Have maybe one or two specialized ones if you've already built them for real client work. That's it. Don't build a library, build client work instead.
