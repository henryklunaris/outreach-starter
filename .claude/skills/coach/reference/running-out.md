# "I've Messaged Everyone I Know. What Now?"

First: congratulations. If you've genuinely done the volume on your warm list, you're further than 95% of people who started this course.

This file tells you what's next. It's NOT more warm outreach. It's a graduation to a different game.

## Step 1, are you actually done?

Before you accept you've run out, answer honestly:

- Have you messaged parents of friends, friends of parents, cousins of cousins, not just your immediate circle?
- Have you followed up on silent threads 2-3 times each over the last couple of weeks?
- Have you asked every replied contact for a referral, even the ones who said "not really my thing"?
- Have you gone through old Facebook, old WhatsApp groups, uni class lists, old team chats?

If any of those is no, you're not out of warm contacts yet. Go do those. See `small-network.md`.

## Step 2, if you really are out

You've squeezed the warm list. The next phase is cold, and cold has different rules:

- Cold needs a niche. You can't run warm-outreach "message everyone" logic on strangers, it won't convert.
- Cold needs an offer, not a conversation. The whole Information Funnel structure flips.
- Cold needs volume tools, scheduling, CRM, lists. Your SQLite tracker in this repo isn't designed for that.

**This repo is warm-only on purpose.** Trying to adapt it for cold will end in a bad hybrid.

## Step 3, go to the Alexander course

Your next move is the Alexander modules on:

- Niche picking. How to pick a vertical you can actually close, not the one you wish you could.
- Cold outreach frameworks. Which isn't ACA, it's problem-first messaging with a specific offer.
- The discovery call script once you're booking from cold.

Come back to warm outreach periodically though. As your network grows (clients refer friends, your presence grows online), more warm contacts appear. Log them here when they show up.

## One more thing

Don't treat "running out" as a failure state. You built 10-30 conversations, some of them will pay off months from now. Referrals don't happen on a schedule. Keep the tracker open, keep logging replies when people come back. The warm channel never fully closes.
